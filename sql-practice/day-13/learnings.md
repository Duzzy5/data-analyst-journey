# Day 13 Learnings

## Biggest Learning

The biggest improvement today was not SQL syntax.

It was learning to convert business questions into SQL queries.

## What I Learned

* Always understand the business requirement before writing SQL.
* Identify whether the question requires a Detail Query or a Summary Query.
* Decide whether GROUP BY is needed before writing the query.
* Choose SQL concepts based on the problem instead of memorizing syntax.

## My Problem-Solving Process

For every SQL question:

1. Understand the business requirement.
2. Decide if it is a Detail Query or a Summary Query.
3. Decide whether GROUP BY is required.
4. Identify the SQL concepts needed.
5. Write the query.

## Confidence Check

✅ I can identify business requirements.

✅ I can distinguish between Detail Queries and Summary Queries.

✅ I can decide when GROUP BY and HAVING are required.

✅ I can translate business language into SQL more confidently.

## Interview Takeaway

Strong SQL developers do not memorize hundreds of queries.

They understand the business problem first and then choose the appropriate SQL concepts to solve it.
